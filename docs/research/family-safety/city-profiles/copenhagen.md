# Copenhagen

**Archetype:** Copenhagen (the reference archetype)
**Mode share:** ~28% (the `m_ref` anchor for the safety-in-numbers multiplier)
**Most surprising finding:** Parents' biggest complaint is *their own bike-handling* (turning a loaded Christiania bike), not infrastructure. Infrastructure is taken for granted.

## Per-segment quality — Excellent

Curb-separated one-way cycle tracks on essentially every major road. Two-way tracks where needed (2.2 m minimum is a *norm*, not a recommendation). City's stated design target: "comfortable for an 8-year-old."

## Network continuity — Connected

Roughly half of Copenhagen schoolchildren cycle to school, often independently, even through the busiest intersections.

## What "protected" means locally

Curb-separated cycle track (one-way, raised from the roadway). Painted lanes are treated as acceptable only on the lowest-volume streets. Mixed traffic only on 30 km/h side streets.

## Beloved family routes

- Fælledparken's children's traffic playground (miniature signals and signs — a rite of passage)
- Superkilen / Nørrebrogade route
- Harbour ring (Havnering)
- Any major commuter corridor — they're all built to the same standard

## Avoided corridors

Few meaningful complaints from local parents about infrastructure. Visiting parents struggle with Christiania-bike handling rather than street choice.

## Vocabulary

*Cycle track* (curb-separated, distinct from a painted lane), *Christiania bike / ladcykel / bakfiets*, *the green wave* (synchronized signals at ~20 km/h), *box turn / Copenhagen left*.

## Routing-model implications

- Near-uniform LTS-1 citywide on the OSM-tagged network
- Reference anchor for SiN multiplier (`m_ref = 0.28`)
