# Tokyo

**Archetype:** Tokyo — slow-everything / legal shared-space (a new archetype)
**Mode share:** ~14%
**Most surprising finding:** E-assist isn't a luxury — it's the enabling technology for the whole system. Two-kid *sannin-nori* mamachari only work at load because of the motor, and the motor is what lets parents navigate Tokyo's hills. Without denchari, the slow-everything model would collapse into "walk the kids."

## Per-segment quality — Not rated (no separation)

Three segment types dominate:
- **Narrow residential streets** (*seikatsu douro*) often inside Zone 30 traffic-calming areas — mixed traffic, cars crawling, de facto shared space
- **Sidewalks**, where cycling is legal for children under 13, riders over 70, and where signed; adults technically should dismount or ride "slowly near the curb"
- **Arterials**, which are hostile, so most riders migrate onto the sidewalk

Painted lanes ("jitensha navi line") exist but are just blue chevrons in the door zone.

## Network continuity — Paradoxically high

Zone 30 blanketing plus a dense grid of sub-6m streets means you can chain calm segments across most wards. The gaps are arterial crossings and riverside trunk corridors — Arakawa and Tama riverside paths are continuous but recreational, not aligned with commute OD pairs. **Elementary schools ban cycling to school in most wards**, which tells you how parents feel about the network despite its coverage.

## What "protected" means locally

It doesn't. Tokyo substitutes social and physical friction: 30 km/h zones, narrow cross-sections that cap car speed mechanically, legal sidewalk refuge, and driver culture conditioned to expect mamachari at every corner. The NACTO (speed × volume) frame actually validates this — at genuine 20–30 km/h with low volume, mixed traffic scores "All Ages." Whether Tokyo hits those numbers consistently is contested: over 50% of Japan's road fatalities are pedestrians/cyclists, and 34% of child KSI 2014–2018 involved a bike.

## Beloved routes / areas

- **Setagaya / Suginami residential grids**
- **Arakawa Cycling Road** (weekend family rides)
- **Shōtengai** like Togoshi Ginza (walking-pace mixed)
- The "mamachari commute triangle" of home ↔ hoikuen ↔ supermarket lives entirely on side streets

## Avoided corridors

- **Kanpachi (Ring Road 8), Meiji-dori, Yamate-dori** — high-speed arterials without usable sidewalks
- Rainy days when sidewalk umbrellas block throughput
- Shibuya/Shinjuku station peripheries at rush

## Vocabulary

*mamachari* (mom-bike, step-through with baskets/child seats), *denchari / denshi-assist* (e-assist: Yamaha PAS Babby, Panasonic Gyutto, Bridgestone Bikke), *sannin-nori* (three-person-approved frame, front + rear child seats, legally required spec), *hoikuen-okuri* (daycare drop-off), *jitensha tsuukou-kanou hodou* (sidewalks signed as bike-permitted), *Zone 30*

## Routing-model implications

- **New cost class**: "shared narrow street with speed-suppression geometry" — treat sub-6m + Zone 30 tag as LTS-2-equivalent
- **Legal-sidewalk-cycling** tags matter: OSM `bicycle=yes` on `highway=footway`
- The SiN multiplier is load-bearing here — at 14% mode share, drivers meaningfully adapt
