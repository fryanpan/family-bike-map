# Seville

**Archetype:** Copenhagen-topology, Berlin-quality
**Mode share:** ~6%
**Most surprising finding:** The 120 km network was built so fast and so cheaply (~€0.27M/km, 2006–2011) that the 2024 €8M reinvestment is almost a second build-out. The legend understates maintenance debt. Also: mode share went 0.5% → 5.6% (2006–2011), triggering a ~90% drop in KSI per million trips (Marqués et al. 2017).

## Per-segment quality — OK, aging

Bidirectional, sidewalk-level, physically separated (curb or raised), mostly green-painted — the "Seville model." But 10+ years on, ABC Sevilla documented potholes, cracks, and surface decay; the city announced €8M in 2024 specifically to repair and extend the network.

## Network continuity — Connected (topologically)

This is the headline finding — the network *is* continuous. 180 km connecting residential neighborhoods to center, built 2006–2011 as a ring+radial rather than scattered segments. Mode share held 6–9% through the 2010s with a dip after 2011 austerity (72k → 55k daily trips by 2021).

## What "protected" means locally

Sidewalk-grade separation (curb step down from pedestrians, curb step up from cars). Closer to Dutch/Danish than Anglo "protected." In the historic center, paths degrade to silver-disc markings on shared pedestrian surfaces — the weak link.

## Beloved family routes

- **Guadalquivir riverside** (Puente de Isabel II → Torre del Oro → María Luisa Park, ~15 km mostly off-street)
- **Parque del Alamillo** (one of Spain's largest urban parks, playgrounds, train ride)
- **SE-30 ring cycleway**

## Avoided corridors

- Historic center disc-marked sections (pedestrian conflict)
- Tram corridors in Centro
- Stretches flagged in the 2024 repair budget

## Vocabulary

*carril bici, vía ciclista, Sevici* (bike-share), *Plan Director de la Bicicleta*

## Routing-model implications

- Network continuity index should be near-best-in-class; per-segment quality (surface) penalized
- **Landmark case study** for the safety-in-numbers effect — the Seville buildout is the cleanest natural experiment available
