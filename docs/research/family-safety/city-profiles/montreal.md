# Montreal

**Archetype:** Berlin-hybrid trending Copenhagen on REV corridors
**Mode share:** ~3%
**Most surprising finding:** Winter cycling retention rose from 6% of summer volume (2009) to ~13%, and Jan–Feb counts on key routes rose 159% between 2015–2017. Montreal families actually ride in February because the REV is plowed before many car lanes. When a lane was withdrawn for winter, residents formed a "human bike lane" in protest.

## Per-segment quality — Good on REV, variable elsewhere

REV (Réseau Express Vélo) axes are the gold standard: 2.5 m wide, concrete-curb separated, two-way, signalized, plowed year-round on the same schedule as roads. Outside the REV, older painted lanes and sharrows persist on many arteries.

## Network continuity — Growing but not yet continuous

The REV is 17 planned axes / ~191 km; several in-service (Saint-Denis/Berri, Peel, Bellechasse, Viger/Notre-Dame), others still under construction through 2025–26. CBC coverage (2024) shows the network doubled in 10 years but residents disagree whether it "connects." Gaps between REV axes force drops onto painted lanes.

## What "protected" means locally

Genuinely protected — concrete curbs, raised medians, signal phases. Not plastic posts. **Winter plowing is treated as the protection standard**; a lane that isn't plowed is treated as removed.

## Beloved family routes

- **Lachine Canal multipurpose path** (flat, off-road, Old Port → Lachine)
- **REV Axis 1 Saint-Denis** (Gouin → Roy, ~9 km, continuous curbs)
- **Peel Axis** connecting de Maisonneuve to Lachine Canal through Griffintown

## Avoided corridors

- Non-REV arteries outside the Plateau — Papineau, Pie-IX outside its SRB section, Sherbrooke stretches without protection

## Vocabulary

*REV* (Réseau Express Vélo), *voie cyclable, piste cyclable protégée, EBN* (Express Bike Network)

## Routing-model implications

- **Winter-plowed flag** as an extra dimension (seasonal cost)
- REV corridors are LTS-1; immediately-off-REV is often LTS-3/4 — sharp contrast routing
