# London

**Archetype:** Berlin-leaning hybrid with one Copenhagen-tier pocket (Waltham Forest mini-Holland)
**Mode share:** ~4%
**Most surprising finding:** TfL *retired* the "Quietway" brand because parents and campaigners didn't trust it — routes labelled family-friendly frequently weren't. The only city in the set that publicly killed a family-branded network tier for failing its own bar.

## Per-segment quality — Good where built

The **London Cycling Design Standards (LCDS, 2014/2016)** include a formal Cycling Level of Service (CLoS) scoring tool and Junction Assessment Tool — arguably the UK's most rigorous family-friendliness framework. DfT **LTN 1/20** sets an explicit "8 to 80" design target (and cites "a 12-year-old child cycling to school"). New Cycleways (C4 Tower Bridge–Greenwich, C3 Embankment) are fully kerb-segregated bidirectional tracks — genuinely Dutch-standard.

## Network continuity — Patchy, improving fast

The old Quietways brand was dropped and absorbed into Cycleways because quality was inconsistent — Quietway 1 through Bermondsey is genuinely quiet, but many Quietways just put signs on rat-runs. Central London has a decent spine; outer boroughs are islands — except **Waltham Forest (the "mini-Holland")**, the one borough with a genuinely connected network.

## What "protected" means locally

Kerb-segregated cycle track is the baseline for new Cycleways — closer to Berlin/Copenhagen than NYC/SF. Wands exist (CS7 Tooting) and are widely criticised as the weakest tier.

## Beloved family routes

- **Cycleway 4** (Tower Bridge–Greenwich) — fully segregated bidirectional
- **Waltham Forest mini-Holland** — 22 km segregated + filtered streets
- **Regent's Canal towpath, Thames Path, Richmond Park** — traffic-free
- **Quietway 1** (Waterloo–Greenwich) — confidence-building for new family riders

## Avoided corridors

- **CS7 (Cycle Superhighway 7) Tooting/Balham** — wand-protected; Upper Tooting Road is London's single most dangerous cycling junction cluster per LCC (11 serious + 18 slight cyclist injuries in 5 years)
- **Old CS2 Whitechapel/Bow** sections
- Any unsegregated "Quietway" on an actual rat-run

## Vocabulary

"Cycleway" (C1–C10…), "Quietway" (legacy, deprecated), "Cycle Superhighway/CS" (legacy), "LTN" (Low Traffic Neighbourhood), "mini-Holland," "filtered permeability," "kerb-segregated," "CLoS," "8 to 80"

## Routing-model implications

- Waltham Forest is a genuinely Copenhagen-tier pocket — per-borough overrides matter
- Legacy Quietway labels should NOT be trusted as protection
- New Cycleway (C*) branding is trustworthy — map to `cycleway=track`
