# San Francisco

**Archetype:** SF (hero corridors, spotty network, "protected" = plastic posts)
**Mode share:** ~2.5%
**Most surprising finding:** The Valencia Street center-running bike lane — branded as protected — is being *removed* and replaced with curbside protected lanes, after parents described the plastic bumper "ending in some places and then all that's there is paint" and watching their 4-year-old drift into the car lane.

## Per-segment quality — Bimodal

A handful of genuinely great pieces (car-free JFK Promenade, Slow Streets that are well respected, Sunset Dunes park, Panhandle path) surrounded by painted lanes and plastic-post "protected" lanes of varying quality.

## Network continuity — Spotty

Connecting the good pieces requires stitching through mixed-traffic gaps. Hills compound this — the Wiggle exists precisely because direct routes are unrideable with kids.

## What "protected" means locally

Plastic posts / flex posts / bollards. Occasional painted buffers. Rarely curbs. Weaker than Copenhagen, Berlin, London, Montreal definitions — this is the reference point for the low end of the "protected" definition scale.

## Beloved family routes

- **The Wiggle** (the canonical zigzag avoiding hills)
- **Panhandle path** (car-free, connects to Golden Gate Park)
- **Page Slow Street**
- **JFK Promenade** in Golden Gate Park (car-free)
- **Sunset Dunes** park
- **Crissy Field**

Rosa Parks Elementary has become a local hub — critical-mass effect. Dorie Apollonio: "There are so many biking families at Rosa Parks now that we are… sort of our own gravitational force."

## Avoided corridors

- **Valencia Street** center-running lane (being removed)
- Hills without an e-bike
- Anywhere with bike-theft risk (parents warn against locking multiple bikes to a single light pole)
- Most SOMA and Market Street adjacencies outside car-free sections

## Vocabulary

*The Wiggle, Slow Streets, JFK Promenade, e-assist, cargo bike, Class IV protected, door zone*

## Routing-model implications

- `cycleway=lane` → **reject** as protection (door zone / plastic-post paint)
- Gradient penalty is load-bearing here in a way it is not in most European cities
- E-assist assumption changes acceptable route choice (tolerable gradient rises significantly)
