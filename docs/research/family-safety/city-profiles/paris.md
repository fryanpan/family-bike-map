# Paris

**Archetype:** Copenhagen-hybrid trending up (via 30 km/h + rue aux écoles, not via lane quality)
**Mode share:** ~5% (rising fast; cycling up 240% 2018–2023)
**Most surprising finding:** A 2025 external ranking placed Paris *above* Amsterdam for child-friendly mobility — driven by school streets and blanket 30 km/h, not bike-infra quality per segment. Paris leapfrogged by attacking speed/volume instead of only building lanes.

## Per-segment quality — Good and rising fast, uneven

New REV (Réseau Express Vélo) axes are genuinely excellent — wide two-way segregated. Legacy painted lanes and some coronapistes are narrower with bollard-only separation.

## Network continuity — Mostly connected on major axes, still patchy in between

Plan Vélo 2021–2026 added ~180 km segregated two-way lanes and made coronapistes permanent; **89% of streets are now 30 km/h**, which does heavy lifting between protected segments.

## What "protected" means locally

Concrete curb or granite sett separator on REV axes; elsewhere, plastic bollards or just a 30 km/h zone. Protection notoriously "disappears" mid-route at intersections and bus stops.

## Beloved family routes

- **Rue de Rivoli** (now buses/bikes/taxis only)
- **Canal Saint-Martin / Canal de l'Ourcq** towpaths out to Parc de la Villette
- **Boulevard Sébastopol** north-south REV
- **Promenade Plantée (Coulée verte)** above Bastille

## Avoided corridors

- **Place de l'Étoile / Arc de Triomphe** roundabout
- **Place de la Bastille and Nation** despite redesigns (big multi-modal swirls)
- **Périphérique-adjacent boulevards**
- Tourist-blocked stretches of Rivoli during peak

## Vocabulary

*coronapiste, REV (Réseau Express Vélo), piste cyclable, zone 30, rue aux écoles, vélorue, sas vélo*

## Routing-model implications

- **Time-of-week / time-of-day edges**: *rue aux écoles* are car-restricted only during school hours
- 30 km/h blanket across most streets means mixed traffic can be scored higher than elsewhere
- REV axes should be treated as LTS-1 backbone
- "Protection drop-out" mid-segment is a common pattern — intersections may need weakest-link reweighting
