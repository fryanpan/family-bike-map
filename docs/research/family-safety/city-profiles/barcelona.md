# Barcelona

**Archetype:** SF with a superilla twist (great destinations, weak through-routes)
**Mode share:** ~3%
**Most surprising finding:** Barcelona builds the low-stress network *backwards* — superilla interiors first (low-stress islands), arterial connectors later. A family can ride safely inside Sant Antoni or around Consell de Cent, but crossing between superilles still means exposed intersections on arterials.

## Per-segment quality — OK to good, very bimodal

Eixample carrils bici on Diagonal, Gran Via, Consell de Cent are proper segregated two-way lanes with curbs. Outside those spines it's painted or shared 30 km/h.

## Network continuity — Patchy, improving

~260 km of lanes, plan to grow 40%. The grid helps — Eixample's regularity means parallel options — but the superilla interiors don't yet connect into a continuous *bike* network; they're low-traffic islands rather than through-routes.

## What "protected" means locally

Raised curb separator on Eixample axes (often between parked cars and sidewalk); elsewhere, paint or *zona 30*. Superilla interiors are pacified by diverters, not bike infra per se.

## Beloved family routes

- **Carril bici along Consell de Cent** (transformed into a linear park/bike axis as part of the Eixample superblock)
- **Passeig Marítim** seafront from Barceloneta to Fòrum
- **Carretera de les Aigües** on Collserola for older kids
- **Sant Antoni superilla interior**

## Avoided corridors

- **Gran Via de les Corts Catalanes** multi-lane sections despite the bike lane (high-speed, bus-heavy)
- **Meridiana**
- **Via Laietana** during its reconstruction-era chaos
- Tight Ciutat Vella lanes where delivery vans and tourists dominate

## Vocabulary

*superilla / supermanzana, carril bici, zona 30, eix verd, camí escolar, Bicing*

## Routing-model implications

- Superilla interiors are first-class **low-stress polygons** — tag them explicitly
- Arterial crossings are the cost surface
- *Camins escolars* (school routes) are doing more for 3–8-year-olds today than the bike lanes are
