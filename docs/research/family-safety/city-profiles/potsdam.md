# Potsdam

**Archetype:** Berlin-variant, shading toward a leisure/Havelland hybrid
**Mode share:** ~20% (SrV 2023 mobility survey)
**Most surprising finding:** Potsdam is **NOT** structurally better than Berlin on infrastructure policy. ADFC Fahrradklima-Test 2022 scored 3.7 (same as 2020, worse than 3.4 in 2012–14), rank 8/40 in the mid-sized-cities category. The perceived "Potsdam is better" effect is explained largely by **geography + scale**: lower traffic volumes, Havel/Sanssouci car-free spines acting as a de facto family network, and small size meaning 2–3 good corridors cover most trips. ADFC Potsdam is simultaneously complaining the network *shrank* in 2022 and cyclist injuries rose 18%.

## Per-segment quality — Good, with a sharp old-town penalty

Outside the historic core, segments are noticeably smoother and more continuous than Berlin's: asphalt paths through Sanssouci/Neuer Garten, wide shared-use trails along the Havel, newer separated lanes on key arterials.

**The penalty is concentrated**: the Altstadt, Holländisches Viertel, and Brandenburger Vorstadt have heavy Kopfsteinpflaster (granite sett cobblestone), which is a real child-bike killer. Citizen petitions on buergerbeteiligung.potsdam.de explicitly ask for smooth cycling strips inlaid into Gutenbergstraße and Mittelstraße.

## Network continuity — Patchy-to-mostly-connected

ADFC Potsdam's 8-Punkt-Plan flags that the network grew only 2.5 km through end of 2022 and then actually shrank. ADFC target is 10 km/year of new paths plus 10 km/year of quality upgrades. Continuity *feels* better than Berlin because (a) the city is small enough that 2–3 good corridors cover most trips, and (b) the park/lake system provides long car-free spines.

## What "protected" means locally

Same German options as Berlin: curb-separated sidewalk bike path, Fahrradstraße (bike-priority street, cars as guests), or car-free park/Havel trail. Plastic-post American-style "protection" is essentially absent. ADFC 8-Punkt-Plan explicitly aspires to "consistent separation of cycling from car traffic where possible" plus elimination of Bettelampeln (beg-buttons) by 2027.

## Beloved family routes

- **Berliner Mauerweg / Havelradweg** around Griebnitzsee → Glienicker Brücke → Sacrow — mostly flat, paved, waterfront, car-free or near-car-free. The canonical Berlin-to-Potsdam family ride.
- **Park Sanssouci internal paths + Neuer Garten → Heiliger See → Cecilienhof** — signed designated bike routes inside the parks (riding only on marked routes per park rules), but those routes are long, smooth, and car-free.
- **Templiner See / Babelsberger Park loop** — flat, paved, lake-hugging, the staple toddler-friendly ride.

## Avoided corridors

- **Breite Straße / Zeppelinstraße** — main arterial, heavy car traffic, repeatedly flagged by ADFC
- **Nuthestraße / B2 approaches** — fast, truck-heavy
- **Altstadt cobbles** (Friedrich-Ebert-Straße, Brandenburger Straße edges, Holländisches Viertel) — avoided not for traffic but for surface; child bikes rattle hard and narrow tires track the joints, especially when wet

## Vocabulary and Berlin contrast

Same vocabulary as Berlin (Fahrradstraße, Radweg, Schutzstreifen, Kopfsteinpflaster), but the felt experience differs: Potsdam's villa-district grid means **the "quiet side street" archetype actually dominates** because traffic volumes are inherently lower, whereas Berlin's equivalent side streets are often rat-runs. ADFC Potsdam's complaint language mirrors Berlin's — the problems are the same, just at lower intensity.

## ADFC Fahrradklima-Test 2022

Grade **3.7**, rank **8/40** in the mid-sized-cities category (Erlangen won with 3.24; national average 4.1). Stable vs 2020 (3.7), down from 3.4 in 2012–14. Worst sub-scores: bike theft (5.0), sense of security, lane width, conflicts with drivers.

## Routing-model implications

- **Do not over-credit Potsdam as a "solved" city** — its advantage is geography + scale, not policy execution
- Cobble penalty (`surface=sett`, `surface=cobblestone`) remains load-bearing in Altstadt — the biggest family hazard locally
- Park/Havel spines should be boosted as first-class LTS-1 corridors
- Inherits Berlin archetype defaults; minor overrides: higher network continuity index (because parks substitute for the bike network), slightly better surface distribution outside Altstadt
- SiN multiplier at 20% mode share → ~1.09× (barely below Berlin's 1.11×)
