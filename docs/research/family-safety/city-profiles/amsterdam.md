# Amsterdam

**Archetype:** Amsterdam (beyond Copenhagen — new failure mode is bike congestion, not cars)
**Mode share:** ~35%
**Most surprising finding:** #1 parent complaint is bike congestion and unpredictable tourists/scooters on the busiest fietspaden, not cars.

## Per-segment quality — Excellent

CROW-designed fietspaden, smooth asphalt, forgiving curb radii, 30 km/h default on residential streets. Fietsstraten ("auto te gast") are the gold standard — cars are literally labeled as guests.

## Network continuity — Connected

This is the defining feature. The low-stress network is genuinely citywide; gaps are exceptions.

## What "protected" means locally

Physically separate fietspad at a different grade from the roadway, or a fietsstraat where motor volume is engineered down (not just signed).

## Beloved family routes

- Amstel riverside south to Ouderkerk aan de Amstel
- Vondelpark loops
- Amsterdamse Bos
- Quiet kades in Zuid (Reinier Vinkeleskade, Jan van Goyenkade, Pieter Lastmankade)

## Avoided corridors

- Centrum tourist arteries (Damrak, Rokin) — not because infra is bad but because bike congestion + distracted pedestrians is the real hazard
- Pedestrian-only Kalverstraat/Spuistraat (illegal to cycle)
- Tram-track streets like Leidsestraat

## Vocabulary

*fietsstraat, fietspad, woonerf, auto te gast, kruispunt, OV-fiets*

## Routing-model implications

- Optimize for bike *congestion* avoidance, not car avoidance — a new signal no other city needs
- `fietsstraat` (often `bicycle_road=yes`) is the canonical LTS-1 tag here
