# Taipei

**Archetype:** Recreational trunk + hostile capillaries (hybrid SF / Berlin)
**Mode share:** ~4%
**Most surprising finding:** **The flood wall is the single most important piece of Taipei bike infrastructure.** It created the car-free riverside network accidentally, as a monsoon defense. Routing for families becomes a floodgate-selection problem: which gate gets you closest to your destination with the least on-street exposure.

## Per-segment quality — Bimodal

Riverside paths are genuinely LTS-1: wide, paved, car-free. On-street is LTS-3/4. The infamous **Dunhua bike lane** (NT$60M) was removed after design failure and crashes — set the template for what not to do. Bikes share the outer "scooter lane" with 125cc scooters whose riders "don't indicate or look before switching lanes." Sidewalk cycling is legal on wider sidewalks and is what most families actually do.

## Network continuity — Low for transportation

The 111 km riverside system along Tamsui/Keelung/Xindian/Jingmei is continuous along the rivers but sits behind two-story flood walls, reachable only through specific floodgates. Once you exit a floodgate you're in street traffic. Great for weekend rides, weak for school/work OD pairs that don't parallel a river.

## What "protected" means locally

Physical separation on riverside paths (by flood wall); on-street "protection" is paint plus the scooter-lane shared envelope. No meaningful protected intersection standard.

## Beloved routes / areas

- **Guandu–Tamsui** (left bank, flat, MRT bookends)
- **Dadaocheng wharf to Bitan** via Xindian River
- **Rainbow Bridge / Raohe** via the LOVE-sculpture floodgate

## Avoided corridors

- **Dunhua N/S** (post-removal)
- **Zhongxiao E Rd, Xinyi arterials** at rush
- Any bridge crossing that forces merging into Minquan E Rd traffic

## Vocabulary

*YouBike / U-bike* (dock share, YouBike 2.0), *heibin zixingche dao* (riverside bikeway), *jiche dao* (scooter lane — the de facto bike lane), floodgate / evacuation-gate access points

## Routing-model implications

- **Floodgates as mandatory transfer nodes** between LTS-1 riverside and LTS-3/4 streets — first-class routing entities
- Family routes should prefer the river trunk + shortest hostile-street tail
- Scooter-lane sharing is a unique cost class
