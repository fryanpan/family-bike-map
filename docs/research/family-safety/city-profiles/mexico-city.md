# Mexico City (CDMX)

**Archetype:** Bogotá-like in periphery, Berlin-like in core
**Mode share:** ~2%
**Most surprising finding:** **Enforcement reliability beats hardware tier.** Parents trust plastic curbs on Reforma more than bollards on Insurgentes, because Insurgentes is routinely invaded by taxis — even though Insurgentes' bollard lane cut crashes 51% (2016–2023). The hardware is secondary to whether the lane is kept clear.

## Per-segment quality — OK, occasionally good

A good bike street in CDMX is the Sunday-closed Reforma or a flagship protected ciclovía like Reforma/Chapultepec's permanent lanes. These use plastic curb separators (better than flex-posts) plus "armadillos" — metal LED domes. Quality drops fast off flagship corridors: painted gutters, potholes, illegal parking.

## Network continuity — Patchy, trending toward connected in central core

SEMOVI reports the ciclovía network grew from 274 km (2018) to 534 km (May 2024), with a 71% increase in daily bike trips (~457k/day). But the network is radial and core-centric: Cuauhtémoc / Miguel Hidalgo / Benito Juárez alcaldías are relatively well-served; the rest of the 1,485 km² city is islands.

## What "protected" means locally

Several tiers coexist:
- **Plastic curb separator** (Reforma) — continuous low plastic curb, the gold standard
- **Armadillos** — metal LED domes, visual deterrent only
- **Bolardos** (bollards/flex-posts) — most common on "emergente" corridors like Insurgentes
- **Painted only / shared lane** — labeled ciclovía but no physical separation

## Beloved family routes / areas

- **Paseo de la Reforma on Sundays** (Muévete en Bici, 8am–2pm, ~55 km car-free)
- **Bosque de Chapultepec** — park-internal paths, go-to for little kids
- **Condesa / Roma side streets** (Av. Mazatlán, Durango, around Parque México and Parque España) — leafy, calm, low-speed

## Avoided corridors / conditions

- **Av. Insurgentes and Tlalpan ciclovías** midweek — documented as taxi-infested and potholed
- Anywhere off the ciclovía network during rush hour — 81% of traffic fatalities 2019–2022 were vulnerable road users
- **Contingencia ambiental** days (Phase I AQI 151–200+) — families explicitly avoid outdoor exertion

## Vocabulary

*ciclovía* (bike lane or Sunday open-streets), *Muévete en Bici, Ciclotón, ECOBICI, biciescuela, ciclovía emergente, armadillo, bolardo, SEMOVI, alcaldía, contingencia ambiental*

## Routing-model implications

- **AQI as a first-class cost layer** (Contingencia Ambiental program) — introduces a dimension other cities don't need
- **Enforcement reliability per corridor** — Reforma scores high, Insurgentes scores low, despite similar hardware
- Altitude (~2,240 m) compounds gradient effect in southern alcaldías (Tlalpan, Magdalena Contreras)
