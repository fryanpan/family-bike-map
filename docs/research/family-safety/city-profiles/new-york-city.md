# New York City

**Archetype:** SF with better greenway spine
**Mode share:** ~1.3%
**Most surprising finding:** NYC DOT in 2025 branded "family-friendly bike routes" — but every one is under 2 miles and loops inside a park or short greenway segment. De facto admission that the connected network isn't there for kids.

## Per-segment quality — OK, highly variable

NYC DOT's Street Design Manual defines "protected bike lane" broadly — separation can be a parking lane, curb, raised median, or flex-post wands. In practice, most "protected" lanes are parking-protected paint; full concrete-curb cycle tracks are rare. Many older lanes sit squarely in the door zone. 9th Avenue (the first US parking-protected lane, 2007) is the high-water mark, with signal-separated left turns — most of the network does not match it.

## Network continuity — Patchy → islands

NYC has built a record ~87 miles of protected lanes in three years, but Streetsblog reporting shows four "priority districts" got zero new protected lanes in 2023. It functions more as corridors + park islands (Hudson River Greenway, Prospect Park, Central Park loop) than a connected low-stress web. No official citywide LTS map is published by DOT.

## What "protected" means locally

Usually parking-protected paint, sometimes flex posts ("wands"). Rarely a true raised cycle track. Closer to SF's definition than Berlin's.

## Beloved family routes

- **Hudson River Greenway** (Manhattan, fully car-free, ~11 mi) — North America's most-used bike path; the default family route
- **Prospect Park loop + Parkside Ave protected lane** (Brooklyn) — NYC DOT's official 2025 family route
- **Manhattan Waterfront Greenway / East River Greenway** — off-street

## Avoided corridors

- **Queens Boulevard** — "paint and plastic" lane constantly blocked by illegal parking and slip-lane turns
- Most crosstown Manhattan streets (door-zone paint lanes)
- Outer-borough arterials in priority districts with no protected infrastructure

## Vocabulary

"Protected bike lane" (often parking-protected), "greenway," "Slow Streets" (pandemic-era open streets), "bike bus," "door zone," "salmoning" (wrong-way)

## Routing-model implications

- Parking-protected paint must be calibrated — call it protection or not? Parents treat it as worse than Berlin curbs; use `reject` unless it has a curb
- Greenways dominate family routes — give them a strong boost
